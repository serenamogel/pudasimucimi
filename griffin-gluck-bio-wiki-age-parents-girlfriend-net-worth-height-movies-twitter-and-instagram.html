Griffin Gluck Bio – Griffin Gluck Wiki

Griffin Gluck born Griffin Alexander Gluck is an American actor. He was born in Los Angeles and his father is a film director and producer whereas his mother as a production assistant and line producer.

Gluck began acting when he went with his older sister, Caroline Gluck, to a summer children’s showcase of Guys and Dolls at the Palisades Playhouse. The first major role he played was at three years old in a short film, Time Out, co-produced by his father and directed by Robbie Chafitz.

His big break came in 2011, when he took the role Michael in the film Just Go with It (starring Adam Sandler and Jennifer Aniston), hereby Griffin received a Young Artist Award nomination. He was later cast as Mason Warner on Private Practice, and was then upped to series regular on the show. When the series was canceled, he joined a TV pilot called Back in the Game from 20th Century Fox TV. It was picked to be series. The show was later canceled in November 2013.

In 2014, Gluck co-starred as Charlie on the Fox series Red Band Society, in which his character, who is in a coma, is the narrator of the show. In March 2015, he was cast in an NBC pilot, Cuckoo, but was not picked for series.

Gluck had his first film lead role playing Rafe Khatchadorian in the 2016 movie Middle School: The Worst Years of My Life, based on the hit novel by James Patterson.

Gluck was nominated for “Best Performance in a Feature Film – Supporting Young Actor” category at the 33rd Young Artist Awards in the year 2012.
