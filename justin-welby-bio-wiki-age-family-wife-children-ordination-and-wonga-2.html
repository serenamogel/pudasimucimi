Justin Welby Business Career

Before Justin became the Archbishop of Canterbury, he worked at French oil firm Elf Aquitaine based in Paris for five years and Enterprise Oil plc in London. He worked in the oil business for 11 years. He was a congregation member at the evangelical Anglican church of Holy Trinity in Brompton, London while working at Enterprise Oil plc in London.

Justin left the oil business as he felt that God wanted him to be ordained. John Hughes, the Bishop of Kensington at first declined his ordination. He was later confirmed by the Vicar of Holy Trinity Brompton, Sandy Millar, and was accepted for ordination.

Archbishop Justin Welby

Justin joined Cranmer Hall and St John’s College, Durham where he studied theology and prepared for the priesthood in 1989. He was awarded a Bachelor of Arts (BA) degree and a Diploma in Ministry in 1992.

Rev Justin Welby Ordination

Welby was ordained as a deacon by Simon Barrington-Ward, the Bishop of Coventry at Petertide on 28 June 1992, and a priest on 27 June 1993 at Coventry Cathedral. Between 1992 and 1995, Welby was the curate at Chilvers Coton and St Mary the Virgin, Astley (Nuneaton).

Justin became rector of St James’ Church, Southam. He was the vicar of St Michael and All Angels, Ufton, Diocese of Coventry, between 1995 and 2002. He was appointed a canon residentiary of Coventry Cathedral in 2002.

Justin was also appointed as the co-director for the international ministry at the International Centre for Reconciliation in the same year. Three years later, he was appointed sub-dean and Canon for Reconciliation Ministry. In December 2007, he was appointed Dean of Liverpool.
